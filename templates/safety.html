SAFETY PRECAUTIONS

  1. Stay Calm: Try to remain as calm as possible. Panic can impair judgment and decision-making.
  2. Call Emergency Services: Dial the emergency services number immediately to report the fire. Provide your location and any relevant details about the fire's location and size.
  3. Evacuate Immediately: If you are in the path of the fire, leave the area as quickly and safely as possible. Follow designated evacuation routes, if available.
  4. Follow Authorities' Instructions: Stay tuned to local radio or TV stations for updates and evacuation orders. Follow the instructions given by emergency personnel.
  5. Inform Others: If you know of others in the area, inform them about the fire and the need to evacuate if they are unaware.
  6. Wear Protective Clothing: If you must evacuate through smoke or fire, wear long-sleeved shirts, long pants, a hat, goggles, and a handkerchief or filter mask to protect your lungs.
  7. Close Windows and Doors: If you leave your home, close all windows and doors to prevent drafts. Open dampers in fireplaces but close fireplace screens.
  8. Shut Off Gas: Turn off the gas supply at the source before leaving your property if authorities advise you to do so.
  9. Take Important Documents: If time allows, take important documents like identification, insurance papers, medical records, and prescriptions.
  10. Create a Safety Zone: If escape routes are cut off and you cannot evacuate, create a safety zone in a cleared area away from trees, brush, and other flammable materials. Clear a space down to bare soil around you.
  11. Stay Low: If you are trapped in smoke, get down as low to the ground as possible, especially if you are having trouble breathing.
